# Nike Competitive Analysis

## Overview

Nike, Inc. is the world's largest supplier of athletic shoes and apparel. Founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports, it officially became Nike in 1978.

## Key Metrics (2025)

| Metric | Nike | Adidas | New Balance |
|--------|------|--------|-------------|
| Revenue | $51.2B | $24.3B | $6.5B |
| Market Share (Athletic Footwear) | 38% | 15% | 10% |
| DTC Revenue % | 44% | 38% | 25% |
| Number of Stores | 1,046 | 1,892 | 226 |

## Strengths

- Strongest brand recognition in athletic wear globally
- Massive athlete endorsement portfolio (LeBron, Mbappe, Serena)
- Leading DTC digital platform (Nike App, SNKRS)
- Innovation pipeline (Flyknit, Air Max, Vaporfly)

## Weaknesses

- Premium pricing limits access in emerging markets
- Heavy dependence on North American market (40% of revenue)
- Recent inventory management challenges
- Declining cultural relevance among Gen Z (vs. New Balance, Asics)

## Opportunities

- Women's athletic wear segment growing 12% YoY
- Running boom post-pandemic still strong
- AI-powered personalization in DTC channels
- Sustainability as brand differentiator (Move to Zero)

## Threats

- On Running and Hoka capturing premium running market
- Adidas resurgence with Samba/Gazelle retro trend
- Economic downturn impacting discretionary spending
- Counterfeit products in digital marketplaces
