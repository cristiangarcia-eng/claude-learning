# Product Launch Brief: Nike ZephyrKnit

## Product Overview

**Product Name:** Nike ZephyrKnit
**Category:** Running shoes
**Price Point:** $185 retail
**Launch Date:** August 15 (back-to-school / fall running season)

## Product Details

The ZephyrKnit is a mid-distance running shoe built with Nike's new EcoWeave upper, made from 80% recycled ocean plastics. The sole uses Nike React foam (30% plant-based). The shoe weighs 8.2 oz (men's size 10), comparable to the best lightweight trainers on the market.

**Key Features:**
- EcoWeave upper: recycled ocean plastics, breathable, lightweight
- React Eco sole: 30% plant-based foam, same energy return as standard React
- Carbon-neutral manufacturing (offsets verified by third party)
- Packaging is 100% recycled cardboard, no plastic wrapping
- Available in 4 colorways at launch, including one using undyed recycled fabric

## Target Audience

- **Primary:** Environmentally conscious runners, ages 25-40
- **Secondary:** Casual fitness consumers who value sustainability
- **Tertiary:** Gift buyers during back-to-school season

## Competitive Context

- Adidas has the Ultraboost Parley line (recycled ocean plastic since 2016)
- Allbirds positions as the sustainability-first shoe brand
- On Running launched a fully recyclable shoe (Cyclon subscription model)
- New Balance has made limited sustainability claims compared to competitors

## Key Messages (Draft)

1. "Performance without compromise" — the ZephyrKnit matches or beats competitors on weight, cushioning, and durability while being the most sustainable option
2. "From ocean waste to personal best" — the origin story of the recycled materials
3. "Every step lighter" — double meaning (lightweight shoe + lighter environmental impact)

## Budget and Channels

- Total launch marketing budget: $2.4M
- Channels: Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, email, Nike App, in-store displays
- Influencer partnerships: 12 mid-tier running influencers (50K-500K followers)
- Athlete feature: Two Nike-sponsored marathon runners for credibility

## Success Metrics

- 50,000 pairs sold in first 30 days
- 15% of buyers are new-to-Nike customers
- Social media: 10M impressions in launch week
- Email click-through rate above 4.5%
- Earn at least 3 major press placements (Runner's World, Wired, etc.)
