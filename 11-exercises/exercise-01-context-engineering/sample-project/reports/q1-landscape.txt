Nike Competitive Analysis -- Q1 2025 Landscape Summary
=======================================================

Prepared by: Competitive Strategy Team
Date: April 1, 2025
Period: January - March 2025

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Nike maintained its leading position in Q1 2025 with 27.3% market share in
North America, though year-over-year growth slowed to 1.2%. The biggest
story this quarter is the continued rise of Orbit Sports, which gained
share in every region and grew 11.3% YoY in Asia Pacific.

KEY COMPETITOR MOVES

Orbit Sports:
- Launched "FlexStride" running line in February targeting mid-price segment
- Expanded DTC online presence with same-day delivery in 12 metro areas
- Signed three collegiate athletic programs as brand partners
- Growing fastest in Asia Pacific (11.3% YoY)

Velocity Gear:
- Released Q1 sustainability report claiming 40% recycled materials
- Opened 8 new flagship stores in European capitals
- Partnership with marathon training app "RunPath" for co-branded content
- Steady growth across all regions (3-5% YoY)

Apex Athletics:
- Viral social media campaign drove 22.6% growth in marketplace channel
- Strongest with 18-25 demographic; lifestyle positioning is working
- Expanded into casual wear with "Street Series" collection
- Growing fast but still below 12% share in any region

Summit Run:
- Raised prices 8% on premium trail line with minimal volume impact
- Highest margins in the category (estimated 62% gross margin)
- Limited distribution strategy keeps brand perception premium
- Slowest growth but most profitable per unit

CHANNEL TRENDS

- DTC Online growing for all brands (Nike +8.2%, Orbit +14.5%)
- Wholesale declining for all brands (industry shift to direct)
- Marketplace channels surging (Apex +22.6% leads)
- DTC Stores flat to slightly positive

SO WHAT?

Nike's core risk is not losing the top spot -- it is losing momentum.
Orbit Sports is growing 5-10x faster in key metrics. Apex Athletics owns
the youngest demographic. Nike needs a stronger DTC and marketplace
strategy to maintain leadership in the next 2-3 years.
